About Me

I am a PhD Zoology candidate with a research background in insect eco-physiology, spanning both applied pest management and fundamental thermal biology. My work focuses on how insects interact with their environment, with particular emphasis on the physiological mechanisms underlying thermal responses and their implications under climate change. I have experience in experimental design, insect rearing, physiological assays, and statistical analysis in R. I am also engaged in science communication across academic and public audiences, with a focus on accessibility and impact.

Current Research

  • Thermal performance of invasive insects
  • Temperature effects on life-history traits
  • Climate change biology in insects

Future Research

  • Climate change impacts on species interactions
  • Expanding thermal biology experiments
